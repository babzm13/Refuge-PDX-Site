Be a Non-Player Character

Become an NPC and Bring the World to Life!

New players are often suggested to try out NPCing for their first few games, but it is not required. NPCs get the opportunity to learn the rules more slowly and have teachers along the way in the form of the NPC Guild members and the Plot Team.

NPCs ARE the world. They make the world come to life by playing the bankers, traders, farmers, nobles, and peasants that the PCs interact with. They are also the bad guys - monsters, goblins, pirates, fae, and others. They present the problems for the PCs to solve, under the guidance of the Plot Team. The Plot Team writes the stories, then instruct the NPCs on the salient points, and let the NPCs bring the stories to life through role play and combat. NPCs learn the rules by having NPC Guildies - NPCs who have made a 1-year commitment to the game, and who have shown themselves to be either good RPers, good combat people, or both - and Plot help guide them in the important parts, and PCs will help on the field.

NPCs have a much lower barrier of entry - they just need a good attitude and some black clothes!

If this sounds appealing to you, here are some things to do to get ready for NPCing:

  1. Reach out to the New Player Representative. The New Player Rep has a ton of resources available that can help you get ready for your first game. They are the first tool in your toolbelt for getting ready for your first event. They aren't a replacement for anything else in this list, but they are certainly a huge asset to your process and can answer all your questions!
  2. Skim the rulebook. This is an optional piece for NPCs, but you'll be less confused at the beginning if you know some of the skills in the rulebook. The rulebook (linked above under "Rulebooks" in the menu) is the source of all the most important information in the game. You don't need to worry about classes or species, just skim the skills and you should be in a good place to begin.
  3. Create your blank character on the online database. The online db, also known as the RDB, is where you build the character you can eventually play in game. You don't need to buy any skills, and can choose a class and species at random. They can all be changed later. You can also choose a name if you want, or just name it with a placeholder. That can also be changed later. You will also register for your first event here. NPCs need to do both of these things to be able to play! Please feel free to ask questions if you have them about any part of this process.
  4. You'll probably also want to skim the FAQs. This document will answer a lot of the questions you probably have getting ready for your first event, including things like what to pack.
  5. Finally, be sure to introduce yourself on our social media pages! You can find them on our Contact Us pages. Join us and make some friends before you come!

Some specific NPC things to add to the packing list:

  • Black clothes. All NPCs wear black under their garb. Garb is provided for you by the game, but if you have something you want to wear, you're welcome to bring it!
  • Extra socks. Your feet will get wet and cold, and clean warm socks are just the best to have.
  • Food, if you don't want to be on the NPC meal plan. The NPC meal plan feeds NPCs Friday dinner through Sunday breakfast for around $25. If you have allergies, we can usually accommodate them, if you let us know early enough, and we generally have snacks around Monster Camp too.

And that's about it. Feel free to reach out with your questions to the New Player Rep or anyone on our Social Media pages.
